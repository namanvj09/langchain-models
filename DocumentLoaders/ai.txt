What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of creating computer systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence — such as understanding language, recognizing images, solving problems, learning from data, and making decisions.

Main Types of AI
1. Narrow AI

Designed for specific tasks.
Examples:

Voice assistants like Siri
Recommendation systems from Netflix or YouTube
Spam filters and chatbots

Most AI today is Narrow AI.

2. General AI (AGI)

A hypothetical AI that can think and learn across many domains like a human. AGI does not yet exist.

3. Generative AI

AI that creates new content such as text, images, music, video, or code.
Examples:

ChatGPT
GitHub Copilot
Midjourney
How AI Works

AI systems are usually trained on large amounts of data using algorithms and machine learning models.

Key concepts:

Machine Learning (ML): Systems learn patterns from data.
Deep Learning: Uses neural networks inspired by the human brain.
Natural Language Processing (NLP): Helps AI understand and generate language.
Computer Vision: Enables image and video understanding.
Common AI Applications
Healthcare diagnostics

Benefits of AI
Automates repetitive work
Improves efficiency and accuracy
Enables faster decision-making
Enhances customer experiences

Challenges & Risks

Simple One-Line Definition

AI is the simulation of human intelligence by machines to learn, reason, and solve problems.
